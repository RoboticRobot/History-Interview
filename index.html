This is a Transcript between me, and... um, someone from New France.

Me: Ok, let me get this straight. You were frozen, in a chunk of ice, then revived.

Citizen: Apparently.

Me: Ok then. I am going to interview you, please answer these questions as best you can.

Citizen: Ok.

Me: Maybe you can tell me about your Daily Life in New France.

Citizen: Well, the food was... interesting. We got to eat things here that some people in France had never heard even of!

Me: Now, we have the ability to get whatever food we want, thanks to supermarkets, Therefore removing our dependence on growing our own food. So, What did you do in your free time?

Citizen: Generally, we would go to a play, or read a book. That wasn't too easy, as most books were published in France.

Me: Today, we have a wealth of entertainment, right at our finger-tips! The Internet. (Shows iPhone, plays Flappy Bird.) Next, how did you deal with diseases?

Citizen: When we got sick, mostly because of scurvy, we would see a doctor. Sometimes the Natives would help us.

Me: Today, we have a great health infrastructure, and most diseases can be stopped from doing much damage to us. We suffer from disease incredibly less often today. What were your living conditions like?

Citizen: We lived in a variety of houses. Myself, I lived in a small cabin.

Me: Today, we expect amazing conditions compared to what you were used to. We have heaters, ACs, thermostats, specially engineered beds, all for more comfort!

Citizen: We had heaters!

Me: Yeah, but they were pathetic! I mean, feel the temperature in this room! Have you ever felt this cozy in CANADA, IN THE WINTER?!

Citizen: I guess not.

Me: On the other hand, you weren't fracking and killing the Earth, were you?

Citizen: What's Fracking?

Me: Never mind. Did you enjoy your life in New France?

Citizen: I guess I did.

Me: Thank you, that will be all.

END OF TRANSCRIPT
